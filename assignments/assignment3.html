The Boom of Live Music – Explanatory Infographic

A long-form infographic that combines multiple datasets and four explanatory theories to tell a cohesive story about why live music is booming in the era of streaming.

Full infographic preview. The graphic scrolls inside this panel while the written case study unfolds on the right.

Overview

This project investigates why live music is booming at a time when streaming dominates how people listen to music. Rather than focusing on a single cause, the infographic weaves together several datasets and four complementary theories, moving from global revenue trends to format shifts, post-pandemic dynamics, generational change, and the role of social media in amplifying demand for concerts and tours.

The result is a long-form, poster-style piece designed to be read top-to-bottom, using hierarchy, color, and visual rhythm to guide the reader through the argument.

Narrative Structure

The infographic is structured in three main layers:

  • Headline & context. The title and subtitle frame the core question: why is live music booming in the era of cloud and streaming?
  • Big-picture trend. The first chart shows global live vs recorded music revenues over time, establishing that live music has grown steadily and becomes the foundation for the rest of the story.
  • Four explanatory theories. The bottom section presents four panels, each representing a different theory supported by its own chart and visual treatment.

This structure lets readers first understand the overall pattern, and then explore more nuanced explanations without getting lost.

The Four Theories

Each of the four numbered panels highlights one explanation for the live-music boom:

  • The Format Shift. Streaming reduced artists’ income from recordings and pushed them toward live performances as their primary revenue source.
  • The Post-Pandemic Effect. COVID-19 created a sharp drop followed by an exceptionally strong rebound, as pent-up demand and rescheduled tours flooded the market.
  • New Generation Mindset. Younger generations entering the workforce show different spending preferences, placing higher value on experiences over ownership.
  • Social Media Virality. Digital culture, fandoms, and social platforms amplify visibility and fear of missing out, driving more people to attend shows.

The closing statement at the bottom of the infographic synthesizes these ideas: regardless of which theory is most important, the conclusion is clear – live music is booming.

Visual & Design Choices

The visual language is inspired by concert lighting and stage design: deep blues and purples, strong contrast, and neon accents. This creates a clear connection between the data and the atmosphere of live shows.

Key design decisions include:

  • using a consistent grid to align charts, annotations, and numbered theory panels,
  • maintaining a limited color palette to avoid visual noise,
  • using large, legible typography for titles and theory labels, and
  • adding subtle glow effects and icons to reinforce the concert theme without overpowering the data.

Tools Used

Flourish / charting tools – for creating the main time-series and area charts.
Canva – for layout, typography, color treatment, and final composition of the infographic.
Spreadsheet tools – for data preparation and basic calculations before visualization.

What I Learned

This assignment was a chance to practice long-form explanatory design: balancing multiple charts, narrative text, and a strong visual theme in a single piece. I learned how to:

  • orchestrate several datasets into one coherent story arc,
  • use hierarchy and spacing to make a dense infographic feel readable,
  • connect visual language (lighting, colors, icons) to the subject matter in a consistent way.

The infographic complements the more playful comic of Assignment 1 and the historical StoryMap in Assignment 2, showing a different side of my approach to data storytelling: highly structured, theme-driven, and focused on synthesis.
