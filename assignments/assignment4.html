Overview

RoboStory is the final project for the “Data Visualization & Storytelling” course. Instead of a traditional slide deck, the piece was designed as a vertical scrollytelling experience hosted as a Canva website. It combines visuals, text, and data-driven insights to explain [brief explanation of what RoboStory is about].

The goal was to create something that feels closer to an editorial long-read or interactive article than a classroom presentation, while still being lightweight enough to share and load quickly.

Narrative Structure

The story is organized in clear sections so that readers can follow a logical arc from context to implications:

  • Opening hook. A strong visual and headline introduce the main theme and why it matters.
  • Context & background. Short sections explain the current landscape and the problem being addressed.
  • Data & evidence. Charts and diagrams highlight key numbers, trends, or comparisons.
  • Implications & future. The piece ends with takeaways, open questions, or scenarios for what happens next.

Each section is designed to be scannable, with clear headings, limited text, and strong visual anchors.

Visual & Interaction Design

The visual language of RoboStory builds on the course’s emphasis on clarity and narrative. Key choices include:

  • using a consistent color palette and type hierarchy across the entire scrollytelling piece,
  • balancing illustration and data visuals so that charts support the story rather than interrupt it,
  • keeping animations subtle and purposeful, so they guide attention instead of becoming distractions,
  • designing for both desktop and mobile readers through large, legible text and full-width blocks.

Canva’s website export made it possible to prototype quickly while maintaining control over layout and pacing.

Tools Used

Canva (Website mode) – for layout, typography, visual assets, and publishing as a scrolling site.
Spreadsheet / analysis tools – for any underlying data work feeding into charts or key numbers.
Image editing tools – where needed to refine illustrations or background elements.

What I Learned

This project was an opportunity to apply storytelling techniques from the course in a more flexible, web-like format. I learned how to:

  • translate a slide-based idea into a continuous scrolling narrative,
  • use section breaks and visual rhythm to control pacing,
  • design with both readability and personality in mind, without overwhelming the data.

RoboStory sits alongside the comic (Assignment 1), the historical StoryMap (Assignment 2), and the concert infographic (Assignment 3) as a complementary example of how I approach data storytelling across different formats and tools.
