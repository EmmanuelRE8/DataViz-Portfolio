In-class Tutorial · Persona Definition

Character Persona – Motivations, Needs & Narrative Role

A creative storytelling exercise focused on defining a character persona to anchor the broader narrative. In this tutorial, the goal was to transform an abstract theme—in our case, AI and labour disruption—into a more relatable and emotionally engaging story by defining “Robo” as a persona.

Persona-definition board created during the tutorial.

Purpose of the Exercise

The tutorial introduced the concept of persona-driven storytelling—a method commonly used in UX design but extremely powerful in data storytelling. Instead of starting with charts or datasets, we began with a character whose motivations, fears, needs, and limitations define how the narrative should unfold.

This approach helps ensure that the story feels human, grounded, and emotionally resonant, even when the subject matter (AI, automation, labour data) is highly technical.

Defining Robo: Who Is He?

In our story, Robo represents artificial intelligence itself—an entity that grows by “consuming” tasks, jobs, and data. Creating a persona for Robo allowed us to translate abstract AI concepts into behaviour, personality, and stakes that readers could understand.

  • Role in story: antagonist and symbolic force of automation
  • Motivations: hunger for data, expansion, adaptation
  • Needs: more tasks to learn from, more computational “fuel”
  • Fears: stagnation, insufficient data, human resistance

Why Personas Matter in Data Storytelling

Defining a persona helps anchor the narrative for three reasons:

  • Clarity: Readers understand what the story is “about” without needing technical definitions upfront.
  • Emotional impact: A character—even a robot—creates empathy, curiosity, or tension.
  • Structural guidance: The persona's needs and behaviours shape the sequence of events and visuals.

What I Learned

This exercise showed how valuable persona work is when designing visual narratives. It allowed us to:

  • turn an abstract policy topic into a character-driven narrative,
  • identify motivations and conflicts that would guide the story arc,
  • shape tone, visual style, and pacing around the persona’s “voice”,
  • avoid flat or overly technical explanations early in the story.

The persona created here directly informed the tone and structure of our final project, RoboStory.
