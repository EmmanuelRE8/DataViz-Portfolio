In-class Tutorial · Story Ideation

Brainstorming & Story Foundations (Miro Mapping)

A collaborative ideation exercise for defining the core elements of a data story—theme, characters, datasets, visual strategy, and delivery method. This tutorial shaped the foundation for Assignment 1.

Brainstorming board created during the Miro tutorial.

Purpose of the Exercise

This tutorial introduced a structured approach to ideation for data storytelling. Before creating any visuals, the goal was to explore possible narratives, characters, emotional anchors, datasets, and delivery formats.

Working in Miro allowed the group to generate a wide range of ideas quickly and visually organize them into meaningful clusters.

Exploring Themes

We began by listing topics that felt personally meaningful or socially relevant. These ranged from sports and entertainment to inflation, healthcare, pets, videogames, workplace dynamics, and more.

Through discussion and clustering, we gravitated toward **concerts and entertainment events**, which later became the foundation of Assignment 1.

Defining Characters

Characters add emotional grounding to data stories. Our brainstorming included:

  • my dog Penny,
  • a random couple,
  • friends and family,
  • fictional personas (e.g., robots),
  • ourselves.

For Assignment 1, we selected **a couple attending concerts**, which helped structure the comic narrative.

Identifying Datasets

We collected potential sources for storytelling, such as:

  • Billboard Boxscore and Pollstar (concert revenues),
  • Google Trends,
  • Statistics Canada,
  • Industry reports (PwC/Deloitte),
  • Social media data,
  • OECD indicators.

This step ensured that our chosen story would be grounded in credible, accessible data.

Selecting Visual Methods

We evaluated visualization options—line charts, bar charts, Sankey diagrams, waffle charts, bump charts, pie charts, etc.—marking those that best suited our selected topic.

The final selection came from aligning visuals with narrative needs rather than aesthetics alone.

Choosing a Delivery Method

Finally, we explored formats such as dashboards, infographics, videos, card games, interactive maps, and scrollytelling. The group selected **scrollytelling**, which aligned with our creative vision for A1.

What I Learned

This exercise reinforced the importance of structured ideation before designing visuals. I learned how to:

  • explore many possible themes before committing to one,
  • use personas and emotional anchoring to guide narrative tone,
  • link datasets to narrative opportunities early in the process,
  • evaluate visual and delivery methods based on story needs.

This tutorial directly supported the development of Assignment 1 and strengthened my process for conceptualizing complex data stories.
